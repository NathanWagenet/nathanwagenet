Waiting is composed of two sets of poetry that I wrote over the course of a couple years in celebration of both Advent and Lent.

The Lent poetry is simple, consisting of one sonnet per day, each one finding God in a different part of life.

The Advent poetry is more complex, being split out into a different theme for each of four weeks, with different symbolic colors and different styles of poetry for each day of each week.

X The Need and the Promise [Advent: Day 1 of 28]

"I will put enmity
Between you and the woman,
And between your seed and her seed;
He shall bruise you on the head,
And you shall bruise him on the heel." - Genesis 3:15

Long ago, in long-lost Eden, Adam, Eve displayed their greed in
Taking fruit from tree forbidden to them by the Lord Most High.
Suddenly, their deed discovered, they were found by sin that hovered,
Waiting at their door to catch them, drag them down to Hell thereby.
There they stood, so full of shame under their Lord's so-loving eye,
Knowing now that they must die.

Now what could they do, so fated? They, now to destruction slated,
How could they escape their doom, what freedom could their actions buy?
But, the Lord all-loving, caring, came to them, and said, declaring,
"One will come, though long-awaited; he'll the knot of sin untie.
He will crush the serpent's head, and underneath the darkened sky,
He will set right what's awry."

X Our Hope Has Come [Advent: Day 10 of 28]

"Now, Lord, You are releasing Your bond-servant to depart in peace,
According to Your word;
For my eyes have seen Your salvation,
Which You have prepared in the presence of all peoples,
A light of revelation to the Gentiles,
And the glory of your people Israel." - Luke 2:29-32

The Promised One, Messiah, comes to earth.
And now, O Lord, I see your great salvation:
A child, a baby boy, whose virgin birth
Can bring the light of God to every nation.
O Lord, I hope in you, for you are Hope;
And now I may depart from here in peace,
For Christ will clear my sin: I cannot cope,
But Christ will take my sin; may He increase.
Salvation comes, not just to Israel,
But over all the world: Christ reigns on high.
Salvation comes; I bid my sin farewell.
O, I am saved! No longer will I die.
For Christ is light to Gentile as to Jew;
O, glory! did you know? He died for you.

X O Oriens [Advent: Day 27 of 28]

O Morning Star,
splendor of light eternal and sun of righteousness:
Come and enlighten those who dwell in darkness and the shadow of death.

Arise and shine, O Holy Light,
And come to those who live in night!
Arise, and with your healing wings
Bring life to fallen, mortal things:
Lift up the sun, and make all bright.

O splendor of eternal light,
Thy glory comes on us in might;
The prophet's voice through ages rings:
Arise and shine.

Thou Morning Star, Who puts to flight
The darkness, and sets all to right,
Send forth that star which guideth kings!
Those three to Bethlehem it brings
That they, like prophet, may then write:
Arise and shine!

X Day 15

I looked for God, and found Him in the smell
Of flowers, smell of earth, and smell of rain:
That petrichor which permeates my cell
Of drywall, stucco, stone, and windowpane.
You find Your way into my sheltered life
And wake me up with sharp and fragrant scents,
For You're the sweetest smell amid the strife
On earth, where fallen man doth pitch his tents.
Now fifteen days of Lent have passed me by
And You have woken me to life from death.
Thy incense filled my nostril, stung my eye;
Thy Spirit has restored my failing breath.
In smells of earth I found You; take me higher,
To fragrances of heaven, to holy fire.

X Day 22

I looked for God, and found Him in depression,
When nothing shines with life and all is gray;
When life is filled with emptiness, oppression,
I know You're near in all I do and say.
I know You're near, and yet, You seem so far,
So far from what I need but never have;
Come fill my life with You, my guiding star,
And heal my wounds with Thy all-healing salve.
On this, the twenty-second day of Lent,
I ask, O Father, can You really heal?
For now, my strength is fading, nearly spent.
O, help me, God; the pain's too much to feel.
I trust You, Lord; do not forsake me now,
But lift me up from lonely pain, somehow.

X Day 39

I looked for God, and found Him on the cross,
His tortured, naked body hanging there
Between the thieves, and suffering such loss;
I cannot comprehend such huge despair.
O Lord, You willingly have suffered all
That I, the guilty one, might now be free.
Your blood runs down, as over hangs a pall:
The black earth quakes, the whole world mourns for Thee.
Just one more day of waiting, O my God!
How long must this go on? Time seems to halt—
As pain is poured out, saving sinners flawed,
Christ hangs there, lifted up: Thee we exalt.
My God is hanging on a cross—what wonder!
How can this be? My world is torn asunder.
